This is a simulation of a converging-diverging nozzle.
It shows how characteristic lines and diamond shocks form. The method 
uses a purely geometric, interpenetrating, instantaious (no energy), source owned particle.
These facitiltate massivly parallel processing for GPU-residency. The particle system is 
enabled by a GPU driven fast parallel particle collision detection method 
(131M p/s) intergated into the graphics pipline. It renders particles at the same time 
it builds the particle occupancy. My view is that integrating parallel computation and
rendering makes Vukan the ultimate solution for science if you want detailed control of
your environment. It does have a learning curve but that looks infinite only at the 
start. I am working on simulations of boyancy (like smoke, fog) with partilces as photons 
for lighting.
