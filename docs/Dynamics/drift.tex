\section{Drift calculations}

Drift reporting compares the current modeled particle momentum to the initial
modeled particle momentum.  The UI reports only the compact particle-system
accounting values.  Wall ghost closure is written to capture files as a
diagnostic, but it is not shown as a UI momentum row.

\subsection{UI Momentum Model}

The UI current total momentum is:

\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{p}_{CurrentTotalMom}
=
\mathbf{p}_{KineticMom}
+
\mathbf{p}_{TotalIntMom}
+
\mathbf{p}_{WallGhostMomLedger}.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}

The terms are:

\begin{itemize}
\item \(\mathbf{p}_{KineticMom}\): the vector sum of real-particle
translational momentum.
\item \(\mathbf{p}_{TotalIntMom}\): the vector sum of particle-particle
internal momentum.
\item \(\mathbf{p}_{WallGhostMomLedger}\): source-owned wall ghost momentum
recorded when an instantaneous wall contact exits.  This is not a global wall
reservoir and it is not used as a dynamics input.
\end{itemize}

Wall particle-side internal momentum and wall ghost internal momentum are not
included as separate UI rows.  For an instantaneous wall ghost, these two
diagnostic vectors are equal and opposite during active wall contact:

\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{p}_{WallParticleInt}
+
\mathbf{p}_{WallGhostInt}
=
\mathbf{0}.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}

Therefore they do not change the UI current total momentum.

\subsection{Momentum Drift}

The UI momentum drift is:

\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{p}_{MomDrift}
=
\mathbf{p}_{InitialTotalMom}
-
\mathbf{p}_{CurrentTotalMom}.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}

Equivalently:

\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{p}_{MomDrift}
=
\mathbf{p}_{InitialTotalMom}
-
\left(
\mathbf{p}_{KineticMom}
+
\mathbf{p}_{TotalIntMom}
+
\mathbf{p}_{WallGhostMomLedger}
\right).
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}

For particle-particle-only interactions, \(\mathbf{p}_{MomDrift}\) should
return to zero, up to numerical error, after all contacts have separated.  For
wall contacts, nonzero \(\mathbf{p}_{MomDrift}\) means the real-particle
momentum has changed relative to the initial real-particle momentum.  Capture
diagnostics then show whether the instantaneous ghost-side wall accounting is
equal and opposite during the wall contact.

\subsection{UI Rows}

The UI rows are:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{initial\_total\_mom}: The initial total modeled momentum of all
active real particles.

\item \textbf{kinetic\_mom}: The current translational momentum of all active
real particles:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{p}_{particle} = m\mathbf{v}.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}

\item \textbf{total\_int\_mom}: The current particle-particle internal
momentum contribution.

\item \textbf{current\_total\_mom}: The current modeled particle-system
momentum:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{p}_{CurrentTotalMom}
=
\mathbf{p}_{KineticMom}
+
\mathbf{p}_{TotalIntMom}
+
\mathbf{p}_{WallGhostMomLedger}.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
The wall ghost ledger is included in this row, but is reported as a detailed
capture diagnostic rather than as a separate compact UI row.

\item \textbf{mom\_drift}: The difference between
\textbf{initial\_total\_mom} and \textbf{current\_total\_mom}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Capture Diagnostics}

The capture files write the UI rows in the \texttt{[momentum\_balance]}
section.  They also write detailed values in \texttt{[momentum\_diagnostics]}.

\begin{itemize}
\item \texttt{initial\_total\_mom\_*}: Component and magnitude form of the UI
\textbf{initial\_total\_mom} row.

\item \texttt{kinetic\_mom\_*}: Component and magnitude form of the UI
\textbf{kinetic\_mom} row.

\item \texttt{total\_int\_mom\_*}: Component and magnitude form of the UI
\textbf{total\_int\_mom} row.

\item \texttt{current\_total\_mom\_*}: Component and magnitude form of the UI
\textbf{current\_total\_mom} row.

\item \texttt{mom\_drift\_*}: Component and magnitude form of the UI
\textbf{mom\_drift} row.

\item \texttt{particle\_side\_internal\_*}: The full particle-side internal
momentum vector, including both particle-particle and wall-contact portions.

\item \texttt{particle\_pair\_internal\_*} and \texttt{pair\_internal\_*}: The
particle-particle portion of particle-side internal momentum.

\item \texttt{wall\_particle\_internal\_*}: The particle-side wall-contact
internal momentum.

\item \texttt{wall\_internal\_*} and
\texttt{wall\_ghost\_internal\_*}: The instantaneous wall/ghost-side internal
momentum for active wall contacts.

\item \texttt{wall\_ghost\_mom\_*}: The source-owned wall ghost momentum
ledger accumulated on wall-contact exit.

\item \texttt{contact\_internal\_*}: The vector sum of particle-side internal
momentum and wall/ghost internal momentum.

\item \texttt{modeled\_total\_*} and \texttt{modeled\_drift\_*}: Detailed
diagnostic aliases that include wall particle-side and wall ghost internal
terms.  These are capture diagnostics, not UI rows.

\item \texttt{pair\_internal\_scalar}: Scalar sum of active particle-particle
contact internal momentum magnitudes.  This is diagnostic only.

\item \texttt{wall\_internal\_scalar}: Scalar sum of active wall-contact
internal momentum magnitudes.  This is diagnostic only.

\item \texttt{contact\_stored\_scalar},
\texttt{contact\_released\_scalar}, and
\texttt{contact\_remaining\_scalar}: Scalar diagnostic totals for stored,
released, and remaining internal contact momentum.

\item \texttt{active\_pair\_contacts} and \texttt{active\_wall\_contacts}: The
number of active particle-particle and particle-wall contacts in the current
frame.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Wall Contact Closure Diagnostics}

Each active wall contact writes additional closure values in its
\texttt{[particle Pn WallCollision]} capture block:

\begin{itemize}
\item \texttt{particle\_wall\_normal\_kinetic\_mom}: the source particle's
normal kinetic momentum relative to the wall contact normal.

\item \texttt{ghost\_wall\_normal\_kinetic\_mom}: the equal and opposite
instantaneous ghost normal kinetic momentum.

\item \texttt{wall\_particle\_int}: the particle-side wall internal momentum
vector.

\item \texttt{wall\_ghost\_int}: the equal and opposite ghost-side wall
internal momentum vector.

\item \texttt{wall\_closed\_total}: the sum of the four wall closure vectors.
For a correctly constructed instantaneous ghost contact, this should be zero,
up to numerical error:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{p}_{ParticleWallNormalKinetic}
+
\mathbf{p}_{GhostWallNormalKinetic}
+
\mathbf{p}_{WallParticleInt}
+
\mathbf{p}_{WallGhostInt}
=
\mathbf{0}.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
\end{itemize}

The wall closure diagnostics show that the instantaneous ghost is equal and
opposite for the active contact.  They do not introduce a persistent wall
reservoir and they do not change the dynamics.
