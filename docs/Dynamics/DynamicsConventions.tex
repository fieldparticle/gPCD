\section{Dynamics Conventions}

\subsection{Purpose}

The project maintains independently selectable dynamics models.  Each model is
a readable Python implementation whose source-complete structure is intended
to transfer to GLSL with minimal translation.

\subsection{Source-Owned Execution}

Each source particle reads immutable position and velocity snapshots,
processes all of its current contacts, vector-sums the planned contact
response, and writes only its own state.  A source must not write a target
particle.

The opposing source evaluation reads the same frame-start geometry.  Pair
compatibility and whole-system conservation must therefore emerge from the
contact rule used independently by both sources.

\subsection{Overlap Area Convention}

Overlap area is a geometric contact property.  For a particle-particle contact,
the source-side and target-side overlap areas are the same geometric area, even
when the two particles have different velocities.  Therefore, once the overlap
area is computed for a source-target pair, the target particle's overlap area is
known from the same calculation.

Velocity may affect momentum transfer, phase, or future position, but it does
not make the instantaneous geometric overlap area different for the two
particles in the same contact.

\subsection{Frame State}

Current contact geometry and relative motion are calculated from the immutable
frame-start snapshot:

\begin{itemize}
    \item current positions;
    \item current velocities;
    \item current radii and masses;
    \item current overlap geometry;
    \item the current source-owned contact list.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Momentum-Field Contact Response}

For each source contact, \texttt{WeightedDynamics} calculates relative normal
velocity, reduced-mass closing momentum, overlap area, and a source-local
overlap-area weight.  The current raw impulse is:

\begin{equation}
J_{\mathrm{raw},k}
=
q_k A_k
\Delta t.
\end{equation}

Compression is capped by the contact's weighted share of source available
closing momentum.  Release is capped by the contact-owned stored internal
momentum.  All planned contact impulses are vector-summed before the source
velocity is written.

Source-local overlap-area weighting remains only in the compression
available-momentum cap.  It does not multiply the physical pair impulse.
Current \texttt{ThreeParticle} diagnostics show exact vector-momentum return
but approximately \(-0.00020547\) kinetic-energy drift.

\subsection{Force-Field Contact Response}

\texttt{ForceDynamics} treats overlap area as a central repulsive pair force:

\begin{equation}
F_{ij}=q_{ij}A_{ij}.
\end{equation}

Every overlapping frame applies:

\begin{equation}
\Delta\mathbf p_i=-F_{ij}\mathbf n_{ij}\Delta t.
\end{equation}

There are no compression/release phases, available-momentum caps, or
internal-momentum ledgers.  The corresponding pair potential is:

\begin{equation}
U_{ij}(d)=q_{ij}\int_d^{r_i+r_j}A_{ij}(s)\,ds.
\end{equation}

The Force contact law is memoryless, instantaneous, and purely geometric.
Each velocity-Verlet timestep evaluates it at frame-start positions and
predicted end positions.  Force depends on the selected position snapshot,
radii, pair stiffness, overlap area, and contact normal.  Relative velocity is
diagnostic only and does not affect force magnitude.  Only particle physical
state, including position and velocity, persists into the next frame.

\subsection{Persistent Contact State}

\texttt{WeightedDynamics} currently stores source-owned internal momentum and
collision phase by target ID.  Current-frame contact slots are rebuilt each
frame; the target-keyed dictionaries retain the persistent contact state used
for compression and release.

\subsection{Diagnostics}

Momentum, kinetic energy, potential energy, and total energy are reported
diagnostics.  Diagnostic calculations evaluate the contact rule but do not
affect particle motion.  Force potential energy is calculated from current
geometry only.  Documentation must distinguish actual vector momentum drift
from scalar internal-momentum ledger totals.  A drained WeightedDynamics
internal-momentum ledger does not, by itself, prove momentum or kinetic-energy
conservation.

\subsection{Boundary Convention}

Force-model walls must use a conservative geometric response compatible with
the model's integration method.  A fixed wall may contribute force and
potential energy to a source particle, but it is not integrated and must not
receive a particle-state write.  Wall forces must be evaluated at every
position snapshot used by the model integrator.

Long-running closed-box energy statistics are meaningful only after wall work
and wall potential energy are included in the same diagnostics as
particle-particle contacts.
