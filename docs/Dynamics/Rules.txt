Rules

1. We can never change the model from overlap geometry. 
2. Capture (CapXXXXXX.rpt) files are located in C:\_DJ\gPCDData\examples.
3. Must always be parallel friendly.
4. Particle velocities in the cfg represent incoming velocity before turnaround; they define the particles' initial momentum.

5. If a particle at t=0 is in a collision state it will have an overlap area. 
	We assume the particle is always in the rebound phase.
	This means that it started a contact at its initial velocity, penetrated to the zero velocity point, 
	and is outbound from the collision. The zero_velocity_point is the point at which direction changes
	from inbound to outbound. The velocity at the current overlap rebound point is the
	velocity at the particles current condition that would have occured if the particle
	had collided naturally from the initial velocity.

6. zero_velocity_overlap_fraction is good for designed natural collisions,
 	but starting collisions need a calculated pair-level A_zero because we do not 
	know how deep into the collision history they already are.
