\section{Document Guide}
The purpose of the outline review process is to evaluate document
organization before detailed writing and editing begin. The review
focuses on logical flow, paragraph purpose, transitions, and overall
presentation structure. Experience has shown that most revisions arise
from organizational issues rather than grammar or technical content.
Consequently, section flow should be evaluated before detailed
sentence-level editing begins.

After writing has begun the outline process becomes iterative.

There are two types of outline:

\begin{enumerate}
\item The document outline which is a working version of the outline aimed at the manuscript author.
\item The review outline which is an outline provided reviewers.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{verbatim}
The format of the document outline is as follows (including the verbatim):

A manuscript-planning template written as a LaTeX verbatim block. 
Each entry represents one future manuscript section and describes 
the intended content of that section before any actual paper text is written.

The structure is:

=========================================================
Section: <Section Name>
=========================================================

    Subject:
        <What this section is about>

    Purpose:
        <Why this section exists in the paper>

    Paragraph Subjects:

        - <Purpose of paragraph 1>

        - <Purpose of paragraph 2>

        - <Purpose of paragraph 3>

The important characteristics are:

The equal-sign banner is part of the format

Every section begins with:
=========================================================
Section: Name
=========================================================
* Everything is contained inside a LaTeX verbatim environment
* This is not manuscript text.
* This is not an outline using \texttt{\section{}} commands.
* It is a planning document.

Subject:
	One or two sentences describing what the section is about.
Purpose:
	One or two sentences describing why the section exists and what role it serves in the paper.
Paragraph Subjects:
	A sequential list.
	Each bullet should eventually become approximately one paragraph in the manuscript.
	The bullets define the logical flow of the section.

* The document is hierarchical
* First define sections.
* Then define paragraph objectives.
* Only after the outline is complete do we write manuscript text.

I am not writing the paper. I am designing the paper's structure at the paragraph level."
\end{verbatim}
