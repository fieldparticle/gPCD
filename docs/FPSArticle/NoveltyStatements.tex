\begin{verbatim}
=========================================================
Section: Potentially Novel Observations
=========================================================

    Subject:
        Observations produced by FPS that may warrant
        further scientific investigation.

    Purpose:
        Document phenomena observed during simulation
        that were not explicitly prescribed by the
        methodology and may represent opportunities for
        future research.

    Paragraph Subjects:

        - Introduce the distinction between validated
          physical predictions and exploratory
          observations produced by FPS.

        - Discuss the emergence of complex macroscopic
          behavior from a common set of particle
          interaction rules.

        - Discuss the absence of phenomenon-specific
          modifications within the FPS dynamics.

        - Discuss the absence of shock-capturing
          algorithms, artificial viscosity models,
          transition models, turbulence closures, and
          flow-regime detection logic.

        - Describe the converging-diverging nozzle
          observations and the emergence of a kinematic
          signature consistent with choking.

        - Describe the emergence of shock-like
          structures within the diverging section of the
          nozzle.

        - Discuss the significance of these observations
          arising from the same particle dynamics used in
          all simulations presented in this work.

        - Discuss the possibility that complex flow
          phenomena may emerge naturally from local
          particle interactions without introducing
          phenomenon-specific governing equations.

        - Discuss the role of FPS as a potential tool for
          exploratory investigation and hypothesis
          generation.

        - Identify limitations of the current
          observations and emphasize that additional
          validation and analysis are required.

        - Identify future research directions motivated
          by these observations.

\end{verbatim}
